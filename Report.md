# Stability and Contradictions in Responses from an LLM to Prompt Variations

## Abstract
This research investigates the effects of prompt formulation on the stability of answers provided by large language models (LLM). The work will concentrate on contradictions and inconsistencies between different prompts.

---

## Introduction
The LLM tends to generate different answers depending on the wording of the prompt. This makes reliability questionable, especially in cases of contradictory responses.

---

## Methods
- 45 prompts (fact-based + reasoning)  
- 4 prompt variations  
- approx. 180 answers  

Analysis:
- semantic similarity  
- contradiction detection with pair-wise comparison  

---

## Results
- higher stability for fact-based questions  
- reasoning questions resulted in more contradictions  
- adversarial prompts led to less stability  
- structured prompts increased response stability  

---

## Discussion
It is evident that the choice of wording matters significantly. Contradictions suggest inconsistencies in model answers.

---

## Conclusion
LLMs can provide inconsistent answers based on prompt variations. However, stability can be improved through structured prompts.

---

## Future Work
